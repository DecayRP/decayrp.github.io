I was born in the bunker. I'm told it was a very big and expensive bunker but I have no basis for comparison.
These few halls and rooms have been all I've known all my life. The only other places I've been were in my own imagination.
After seeing the outside, I can scarcely imagine calling our home 'big'.

The day we opened the door, we didn't know what we would find.
I'd been taught about the infection, but like the outside world itself,
it was hard to understand the scope without seeing it first hand. Never had I imagined anything so horrible.
Danger runs rampant on the surface. Death lurks around every corner. 

In a way, the people are the worst of it. The infected are horrific and relentless,
but they lack the calculated cruelty of human beings.
On the first day we ventured out to explore an unfamiliar world we were followed home.
Armed to the teeth and dressed in strange armor and helmets,
the people who followed us took everything we had, took our bunker, and said they would take us too.
At an opportune moment we tried to run, but only I managed to get away.
I'm alone up here now, unable to go back to the only home I've ever known.

I have to believe that not everyone is like the people who took everything from me. I have to...
